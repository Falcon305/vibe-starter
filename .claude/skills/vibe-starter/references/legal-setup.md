# Legal setup

The legal pages render from `lib/legal/config.ts`. Set these fields to match reality — accuracy is
the whole point.

## Fields

- `companyName`, `legalEntity`: the operating name and the full legal entity.
- `contactEmail`, `address`: a real privacy contact and postal address.
- `jurisdiction`: `"eu"`, `"us"`, or `"global"`. This decides whether GDPR and CCPA sections
  appear. When in doubt, use `"global"`.
- `governingLaw`: where disputes are handled.
- `collectsPersonalData`: true if you collect anything identifying.
- `sellsData`: true only if you sell or share personal data for value. This turns on the
  "Do Not Sell or Share" control. Almost always false.
- `effectiveDate`: today's date.
- `dataCollected`: plain descriptions of what you collect.

## Do not touch by hand

`lib/legal/modules.generated.ts` and `lib/security/csp.generated.ts` are regenerated by the
installer from the modules you install. Each module adds its own subprocessors, cookies, and CSP
sources. If they look wrong, install or remove the relevant module rather than editing them.

## Important

This generates a solid, honest baseline. It is not legal advice. Tell the user to have a lawyer
review the pages before relying on them for a real business.
